# Cuberto Reference Brief for Personal Portfolio

Date: 2026-03-15
Reference: https://cuberto.com/
Method: Playwright inspection + viewport screenshots

## Scope

This is not a cloning brief.

It is a reference extraction for a personal portfolio that wants Cuberto's confidence, scale, and rhythm without inheriting agency information architecture.

Use Cuberto as a motion and composition reference.
Do not use it as a content model.

## What Cuberto Is Actually Doing

### 1. Hero pattern

- Very large statement-driven H1
- Minimal but confident header
- Large white space before the first heavy media block
- One dominant message, one supporting paragraph
- Immediate scale contrast between navigation and headline

### 2. Transition pattern

- Quiet text block after hero
- Large visual object acting as a bridge between sections
- Rounded section shell introducing a dark stage
- Pace change from editorial white to cinematic black

### 3. Featured work pattern

- Projects are the main storytelling currency
- Media-first cards dominate each viewport
- Captions are compact and sit close to imagery
- Grid has enough asymmetry to feel curated, not templated
- Buttons are sparse and only appear after the grid has built desire

### 4. Services pattern

- Another oversized heading resets attention
- Two-column logic: explanation on one side, proof imagery on the other
- Service list is short, not comprehensive
- CTA is delayed until section value is understood

### 5. Footer CTA pattern

- Very large emotional handoff before utility footer
- Contact details are integrated into the visual system
- Footer keeps the same brand confidence as the hero

## Element Inventory

These are the elements worth referencing.

### Global shell

- Minimal top navigation
- Strong logo lockup
- Circular floating contact affordance
- Large paddings
- Very high confidence whitespace
- Black/white section switching

### Typography

- Extremely large sans-serif headlines
- Small crisp nav labels
- Concise supporting paragraphs
- Tight line-height on major statements
- Caption-like project titles

### Buttons and links

- Rounded outline CTAs
- Sparse usage of buttons
- Hover emphasis is subtle and clean
- CTA labels are short and direct

### Cards and grids

- Large rounded media frames
- Image-first project cards
- Staggered grid rhythm
- Card text kept to one or two lines

### Media usage

- Heavy dependence on 3D stills and polished motion
- Media carries emotional weight
- Text does not sit on top of noisy media
- Rounded containers keep the imagery feeling premium

### Motion language

- Smooth, high-frictionless transitions
- Motion is used to scale drama, not to explain content
- Cursor/follower effects add brand personality
- Section entrances feel staged, not simply animated

## What To Borrow for a Hiring-First Portfolio

### Keep

- Big statement hero
- Confident white space
- Large media modules
- Editorial pacing between sections
- Sparse, premium CTAs
- Strong contrast between section temperatures
- Media-led featured work layout

### Adapt

- Replace agency H1 with role-first hiring thesis
- Replace "projects" logic with "proof-backed cases"
- Replace "services" logic with "what I actually do"
- Replace agency contact CTA with resume/contact conversion CTA
- Replace decorative cursor gimmicks with restrained recruiter-safe motion

### Avoid

- Agency navigation model
- Blog-heavy secondary content
- Too many project cards before the role is clear
- Motion that delays CTA discovery
- Media carrying critical meaning without a text fallback

## Home Page Wireframe

This wireframe uses Cuberto's pacing but keeps your portfolio goals intact.

### Block 1. Hero cover

- Left or center giant statement:
  `Technical Support Specialist building calmer systems for escalations, operator workflows, and feedback intelligence.`
- Small eyebrow above:
  `Support-first. Systems-shaped.`
- One support paragraph under H1
- Three inline proof signals:
  `45% lower repetitive volume`
  `35% faster first response`
  `70% less manual ops load`
- Primary CTA:
  `Open resume`
- Secondary CTA:
  `Email`
- Large visual on the right or below:
  poster / still / scroll scene

Goal:
Make the first screen feel Cuberto-large while remaining instantly recruitable.

### Block 2. Transition / thesis bridge

- Short paragraph block on white
- One line explaining the positioning shift:
  support credibility plus systems leverage
- Optional abstract or still-life media object
- Button:
  `See selected proof`

Goal:
Create the Cuberto-style breath between the hero and the main proof stage.

### Block 3. Featured case stage

- Full-width dark section
- Oversized heading:
  `Selected cases`
- Two or three very large featured case cards
- Each card contains:
  case image/poster
  case title
  one-line result
  2-3 proof chips
  `Open case study`

Goal:
Use Cuberto's project-gallery energy, but with outcome-first case packaging.

### Block 4. What I actually do

- White section reset
- Big heading:
  `What I actually do`
- Three service-like pillars:
  `Escalation systems`
  `Operator tooling`
  `Feedback intelligence`
- Each pillar gets one sentence and one proof anchor

Goal:
Translate Cuberto's services block into recruiter-readable capability framing.

### Block 5. Flagship chapter

- One stronger pinned or cinematic block only
- Left side:
  case thesis, role, context, result
- Right side:
  stills / scrub scene / sanitized artifact frames
- CTA row:
  `Open full case`
  `Open resume`

Goal:
Give one memorable scene without making the site motion-dependent.

### Block 6. Resume preview

- White or paper-toned section
- Headline:
  `Resume at a glance`
- Snapshot rows:
  role
  years
  domains
  key metrics
  location
- 3 highlights with direct evidence markers
- Primary CTA:
  `Open resume`

Goal:
Turn the Cuberto rhythm back toward hiring conversion.

### Block 7. Final contact moment

- Large, emotionally confident CTA section
- Big line:
  `If you need support credibility with stronger systems leverage, let's talk.`
- Two actions only:
  `Open resume`
  `Email`
- Small metadata below:
  GitHub
  Location
  Availability

Goal:
End with Cuberto-level confidence but with portfolio-specific conversion.

## Art Direction Brief

### Core direction

Cuberto-inspired scale and confidence, translated into an editorial hiring showcase.

### Emotional read

- clear
- expensive
- deliberate
- modern
- calm under pressure

### Professional read

- not a designer portfolio
- not a generic engineer portfolio
- a support and systems operator with taste, structure, and proof

### Typography

- Display: large serif or high-character editorial face for selected major headlines
- UI/body: clean sans for labels and reading
- Mono: proof labels, metrics, and metadata

Recommended behavior:
- giant display only for hero, section resets, final CTA
- sans for navigation, case summaries, recruiter content
- mono for evidence markers, captions, and timing labels

### Layout

- very large horizontal breathing room
- large section paddings
- alternating white and dark stages
- media blocks should feel oversized
- avoid small-card dashboard density on home

### Color behavior

- light-first
- paper or warm off-white base
- black or near-black stage sections for featured work
- one restrained metallic accent for CTA or proof focus
- accent usage must stay rare

### Motion behavior

- one dramatic hero reveal
- one strong chapter transition
- smooth card hover and route transitions
- no constant floating gimmicks
- reduced-motion path must preserve all meaning

### Image direction

- stills over generic mockups
- close crops
- bold material lighting
- dark-section imagery can be more cinematic
- resume imagery should remain quieter and flatter

### Surface rules

- large rounded corners on media
- cleaner text surfaces
- less glass, more matte
- avoid decorative ambient fog
- depth should come from scale and spacing first

## Implementation Guardrails

- The first screen must answer role, value, proof, and next step within 8 seconds.
- Resume CTA must be visible before any long project browsing.
- Cases must feel like evidence, not just gallery pieces.
- Any Cuberto-inspired motion must be decorative, not essential for understanding.
- Home can feel cinematic. Resume must stay printable, stable, and strict.

## Bottom Line

The right move is not to make "a Cuberto clone for one person".

The right move is to build:

`Cuberto-scale visual confidence + recruiter-first narrative + proof-backed personal positioning.`
